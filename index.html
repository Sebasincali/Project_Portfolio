User Research Deliverables

Comprehensive research findings and insights from our user studies

Survey Results

7 responses collected - Key findings show 100% consent for data usage, 57.1% are seniors, 71.4% are first-generation students, and 100% would likely use an online tutoring platform.

Completed

Interview Insights

Key themes identified: Students prefer tutors who have taken the class at SFSU, want easy appointment scheduling, and value one-on-one sessions. Common barriers include long wait times and lack of specialized knowledge.

Completed

Persona Development

Primary personas identified: Senior Computer Science students (42.9%), first-generation college students (71.4%), and transfer students (71.4%) who need specialized tutoring and easy scheduling.

Completed

User Journey Maps

Key user flows identified: Students typically log on → schedule appointment → attend one-on-one session. Pain points include long wait times (weeks) and difficulty finding specialized tutors for specific majors.

Completed

Pain Point Analysis

Top pain points identified: Long wait times (weeks for one session), tutors lacking specialized knowledge, difficulty finding major-specific help, and busy schedules preventing consistent tutoring access.

Completed

Feature Recommendations

Key recommendations: Video demonstrations from tutors, photo attachments in forums, study groups, group chats by subject, easy registration, and incentives for tutors (pay, credits, research opportunities, TA positions).

Key Insights & Recommendations

🎯 Platform Adoption

100% of respondents would likely use an online tutoring platform, with 42.9% saying "Most likely" and 57.1% saying "Likely".

⏰ Scheduling Issues

Students face weeks-long wait times for tutoring sessions, with one respondent noting it "usually takes weeks to get even one session."

👨‍🏫 Tutor Quality

Students value tutors who have "taken the class before at SFSU" and prefer one-on-one sessions with experienced tutors.

💰 Incentive Structure

To attract tutors, students suggest: payment, credits toward graduation, early enrollment, research opportunities, and TA positions.
